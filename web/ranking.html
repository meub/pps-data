Weighted ranking explorer

A configurable, relative ranking of the 74 in-scope PPS schools across ten factors. Each school gets a 0–100 score per factor, and the final score is a weighted blend — 100 means "tops on all factors you weighted," 0 means "bottom on all of them." The bottom 8 are highlighted as illustrative closure candidates. Weights below the inputs are normalized, so they don't need to sum to 100.

Not a PPS ranking. This is a what-if tool for exploring how different factor weights would reshuffle the list. Methodology is described below the controls.

Factor weights

Higher weight = bigger influence on the final ranking. Adjust any factor between 0 and 100. Direction is pre-set per factor so a higher rank always means "more likely to stay open" — e.g., Seismic ranks lower-cost schools higher; BIPOC and Title I rank higher-share and designated schools higher (equity protection).

Ranking

Methodology

Per-factor scoring. Each factor is mapped to a 0–100 score via one of two methods. Percentile rank (most factors) sorts schools by value, assigns a 0–100 percentile, and averages ties — this compresses outliers and treats the distribution as ordinal. Min-max linear (utilization) uses (v − min) / (max − min) × 100, so a school at 30% utilization scores categorically lower than one at 60%, matching how closure decisions actually weight the tails. Schools missing a value get 50 (neutral) so they're neither rewarded nor penalized.

Weighted score. Final score is Σ (wi × scorei) / Σ wi, so weights are normalized automatically. The table sorts by score descending; the bottom 8 rows are flagged as illustrative closure candidates.

Factor mapping.

  • Utilization → 2025-26 enrollment ÷ 2021 functional capacity. Min-max, higher = higher score.
  • 10-yr forecast → PRC's medium-scenario 2034-35 enrollment ÷ 2021 functional capacity. Min-max, higher = higher score. Forward-looking counterpart to current utilization; closure math hinges on 2034, not 2025.
  • Seismic → remaining retrofit cost (Holmes 2024). Lower = higher score.
  • Bond Investment → composite of completed seismic retrofit status (full / targeted / planned) and post-2000 construction. Higher = higher score (protect recent investment).
  • Isolation → haversine distance to the nearest PPS school at the same grade band (k-8s overlap both primary and secondary). Higher = more isolated = higher score.
  • Boundary Change → any DBRAC 2018 or SEGC 2023 redraw. Yes = higher score (avoid re-redrawing same communities).
  • Housing Growth → Metro 2045 BLI projected new housing units within catchment. Higher = higher score.
  • DLI → has a Dual Language Immersion program. Yes = higher score.
  • BIPOC → % BIPOC students. Higher = higher score (equity protection).
  • Title I → Title I status. Yes = higher score (equity protection).
